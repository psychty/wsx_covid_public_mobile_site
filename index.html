Cases in your local area

The number of new confirmed cases is now available at a very localised area (called Middle-layer Super Output Areas or MSOAs for short) covering communities of on average 7,000 people. This is useful as it means you can see what cases are like where you live or where your friends and family live. We do not get the same detail from the data published at this level compared to wider areas and when there are fewer than three cases in a seven day period in the MSOA, these are supressed.

You can search for any postcode in England in the box below and more detail about cases in that local area will appear.

As some data are suppressed, where there have been very few cases in a 7 day period, it is not possible to calculate how many cases there have been in total in a local area. See Department of Health & Social care guidance on supressing small counts.

Returning to education in 2021

On the 30th December 2020, the Department for Education set out new guidance for schools and childcare settings. Its premise is to enable settings to prepare programmes for testing staff and pupils over the coming weeks using lateral flow device tests.

To suppor this, the guidance indicates that in the week commencing 4th January 2021, secondary schools should only provide education on site to vulnerable children and children of critical workers whilst prioritising remote education to those pupils in exam years and where possible other pupils. The following week (commencing 11th January), remote education should be available to all pupils and all pupils should expect to return to on-site education from the 18th January.

The guidance also set out a 'Contingency Framework' designed as a means of reducing transmission within settings and the wider community in areas of extremely high prevalence of COVID-19. Primary schools in some local authorities are requested to restrict on-site education to children of critical workers and vulnerable children with a review of restrictions by the 18th January 2021.

The map below shows which areas (highlighted in red) will have primary schools remaining closed for all but vulnerable children and children of critical workers.

Early years settings should be available as normal from January 2021. Shielding advice is currently in place in tier 4 areas, and so all children still deemed clinically extremely vulnerable are advised not to attend school.
